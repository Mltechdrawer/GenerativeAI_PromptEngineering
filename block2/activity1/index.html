    Activity 1

    Prompt classification in LLMs

    Objective of the activity

    The objective of this activity is for students to learn to identify and differentiate different types of prompts used when interacting with language models, in particular:

    • Prompts based on trigger words
    • Prompts with Chain of Thought (CoT)
    • Hybrid prompts
    • Poorly or inefficiently designed prompts

    Instructions

    Carefully read each of the following prompts and classify them into one of the indicated categories.
    For each prompt, briefly justify your decision (2–3 lines), explaining which elements of the prompt led you to that classification.

    Possible categories

    • A. Trigger words
    • B. Chain of Thought (CoT)
    • C. Hybrid prompt (trigger words + implicit CoT)
    • D. Inefficient or poorly defined prompt

    Prompts to classify

    Prompt 1

    Analyze the causes of climate change and conclude with a brief reflection on its long-term consequences.

    Prompt 2

    Solve the following quadratic equation using the general formula.
    Reason step by step and explain the meaning of each operation performed.

    Prompt 3

    Tell me things about artificial intelligence.

    Prompt 4

    Compare relational and NoSQL databases.
    Evaluate their advantages and disadvantages and argue in which contexts each model is more appropriate.

    Prompt 5

    Help me decide between two business ideas.
    Think step by step by evaluating initial investment, risks, scalability and return time.
    Finish with a justified recommendation.

    Final reflection questions

    1. Can a prompt contain elements from more than one category?
    2. Why do some prompts produce superficial responses even if they are well written?
    3. Do you think that asking for step-by-step reasoning always improves the quality of the response? Why?

    Deliverable

    Students must submit: - The classification of each prompt. - A brief justification for each prompt. - A final reflection of no more than 150 words.

    Evaluation criteria (indicative)

    • Accuracy in prompt classification.
    • Quality of the justification provided.
    • Appropriate use of the terminology covered in class.
    • Critical thinking in the final reflection.
