    Examples

    Examples trigger words

    Below are three examples of prompts that use trigger words to guide the type of response generated by a language model.
    These examples show how the conscious use of trigger words allows directing the model’s behavior toward analysis, structured reasoning or critical evaluation.

    1 Analytical prompt

    Trigger words: analyze, identify, conclude

    Analyze the following set of monthly sales data.
    Identify the main trends and the possible factors that explain them.
    Conclude with a brief summary of the implications for business decision-making.

    This prompt forces the model to structure the response into phases of analysis and synthesis.

    2 Step-by-step explanatory prompt

    Trigger words: explain, step by step, justify

    Explain step by step how to solve a quadratic equation using the general formula.
    Justify each step of the procedure and use a simple numerical example.

    Trigger words induce sequential reasoning and clarity in explanation.

    Explain how to solve a quadratic equation using the general formula.
    Include a simple numerical example.

    3 Comparative and critical prompt

    Trigger words: compare, evaluate, argue

    Compare relational and NoSQL database models.
    Evaluate their advantages and disadvantages in the context of large-scale web applications.
    Argue in which cases each one would be more appropriate.

    This prompt guides the model toward a critical and reasoned response, not merely descriptive.

    Examples Chain of Thought (CoT)

    Below are three examples that explicitly request step-by-step reasoning to improve the quality of responses in complex tasks.

    1 Quantitative problem

    Step-by-step reasoning

    Solve the following problem reasoning step by step and showing intermediate calculations:
    A train leaves station A towards B at 60 km/h. At the same time, another train leaves B towards A at 80 km/h. The distance between A and B is 300 km.
    1) At what point do they meet?
    2) How much time has passed since departure?
    At the end, include a summary with the result and the units.

    2 Multicriteria decision

    CoT + structure

    I want you to help me decide between two business ideas.
    - Idea A: online store for women's clothing and accessories.
    - Idea B: promoting products online with affiliate commissions.
    Think step by step evaluating: initial investment, risk, scalability, time to revenue, operational complexity and sustainability.
    Return the answer with this structure:
    1. Comparative analysis (criterion by criterion)
    2. Final recommendation
    3. Action plan in 5 steps

    3 Route optimization

    CoT + verification

    I am planning a one-day trip and want to visit five places minimizing total travel time:
    1. Museum
    2. Park
    3. Shopping center
    4. Restaurant
    5. Theater
    Distances (minutes) between each pair:
    - Museum–Park: 10
    - Museum–Shopping center: 15
    - Museum–Restaurant: 20
    - Museum–Theater: 25
    - Park–Shopping center: 10
    - Park–Restaurant: 15
    - Park–Theater: 20
    - Shopping center–Restaurant: 10
    - Shopping center–Theater: 15
    - Restaurant–Theater: 10
    Generate an initial route and then optimize it step by step, evaluating each change before applying the next.
    At the end, provide:
    - Final route
    - Total time
    - Brief explanation of why it is better than the initial one

    Examples of prompts with Priming

    Below are three examples of prompts that use the priming technique to influence the style, level and approach of the responses generated by a language model.

    These examples show how priming allows preparing the model before the main task, improving the suitability of responses to the context and user objectives.

    1 Priming with expert role

    Act as a university professor of computer science specialized in artificial intelligence.
    Explain the basic functioning of large language models (LLMs) using clear and rigorous language, suitable for final-year undergraduate students.
    Finish with a brief summary of the key ideas.

    Priming establishes the role and expected academic level before generating the response.

    2 Priming with structure and style

    Start your response with a brief introduction, continue with a step-by-step explanation, and finish with a simple practical example.
    The topic is: solving quadratic equations using the general formula.
    Use language suitable for secondary education students.

    Priming explicitly defines the structure and style of the response.

    3 Priming with usage context

    Imagine you are advising a technology company that wants to adopt ChatGPT in its internal processes.
    Describe the advantages, limitations and risks of its use in a professional environment.
    The response should be aimed at decision-makers and not exceed 400 words.

    Priming contextualizes the situation and conditions the approach of the response.

    Examples of One-shot and Few-shot prompting

    Below are examples of prompts that use one-shot and few-shot prompting techniques, providing one or several prior examples to guide the model’s behavior.

    These examples illustrate how one-shot and few-shot prompting allow shaping the model’s behavior through examples, without the need for explicit rules.

    1 One-shot

    with a single example

    Correct and improve the wording of the following text, making it more formal.

    Example:
    Original text: "ola profe, no puedo ir a clase mañana"
    Corrected text: "Hola profesor, no podré asistir a clase mañana."

    Now correct this text:
    "buenas tarde. quiero quitarme de la linea del tfno por que ya no uso mas"

    The model is given a single example that acts as a reference for the task.

    2 One-shot

    format and style

    Summarize the following content following the format of the example.

    Example:
    Original text: Photosynthesis is the process by which plants...
    Summary: Photosynthesis allows plants to produce their food using sunlight.

    Text to summarize:
    Artificial intelligence is a field of computer science that studies...

    One example guides the level of synthesis and style of the response.

    3 Few-shot

    multiple examples

    Classify the following prompt according to its type.

    Examples:
    Prompt: "Analyze the causes of climate change."
    Classification: Trigger words

    Prompt: "Solve this problem reasoning step by step."
    Classification: Chain of Thought

    Prompt: "Tell me things about plants."
    Classification: Inefficient prompt

    Now classify this prompt:
    "Compare two programming languages and argue which one is more suitable for data science."

    Multiple examples allow the model to infer the classification pattern.

    4 Few-shot

    consistent role and style

    Act as a motivational assistant for university students.

    Examples:
    Input: "I feel unmotivated with the subject."
    Response: "It's normal to feel this way sometimes, but remember everything you have already achieved. Keep going."

    Input: "I don't think I can pass the exam."
    Response: "Trust your effort. You have overcome challenges before and this will not be different."

    Now respond to:
    "I feel like I am not making progress on the project."

    Few-shot reinforces tone, role and response style.

    Example Tree of Thoughts (ToT)

    1 Tree of thoughts

    Solve the following problem by exploring multiple possible approaches. Organize your ideas as a reasoning tree: - Generate several options at each step - Briefly evaluate each one - Continue developing the most promising ones Finally, provide the best solution found.

    Example Visualization of Thoughts (VoT)

    1 Visualization of thoughts

    Analyze the problem by creating a visual representation of your thoughts. Organize the information as if it were a diagram or concept map: - Identify the main elements - Show relationships between them - Use a clear hierarchical structure Then, explain the final solution.

    Examples of prompts with format

    1 Specify the output format

    Plain text: I can generate responses in simple text format, which is the standard output format in most conversations.
    Lists and bullet points: I can create numbered or bulleted lists to organize information in a more structured way.
    Tables: I can generate simple tables with rows and columns to present data in an organized manner.
    Source code: If you need examples of code in popular programming languages, I can generate source code in formats such as Python, Java, C++, etc.
    Citations and references: If you need citations or references in your responses, I can provide them in the appropriate format, such as APA or MLA style.
    Summaries: I can summarize long information or extensive documents into a more concise and easy-to-understand format.
    Long paragraphs: If you prefer more detailed and expansive responses, I can generate long paragraphs with complete explanations.
    Translations: I can translate text from one language to another if needed.
    Summaries and extracts from URLs: I can provide summaries or extracts of information contained in valid URLs, as long as they are not subscription-based or private sources.
