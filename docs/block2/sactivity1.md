# Solution · Activity 1 - prompt classification

## Classification and justification of the prompts

---

### Prompt 1
> Analyze the causes of climate change and conclude with a brief reflection on its long-term consequences.

**Classification:**  
**A. Trigger words**

**Justification:**  
The prompt uses trigger words such as *analyze* and *conclude*, which guide the expected type of response. However, it does not explicitly request step-by-step reasoning or the exposition of the internal process, so it cannot be considered Chain of Thought.

---

### Prompt 2
> Solve the following quadratic equation using the general formula.  
> Reason step by step and explain the meaning of each operation performed.

**Classification:**  
**B. Chain of Thought (CoT)**

**Justification:**  
The prompt explicitly requests to *reason step by step* and explain each operation, which leads the model to produce a detailed sequential reasoning process. This is a clear example of explicit Chain of Thought.

---

### Prompt 3
> Tell me things about artificial intelligence.

**Classification:**  
**D. Inefficient or poorly defined prompt**

**Justification:**  
The prompt lacks clarity, specificity and a defined objective. It does not provide context nor define the expected type of response, which usually leads to vague or unhelpful answers.

---

### Prompt 4
> Compare relational and NoSQL databases.  
> Evaluate their advantages and disadvantages and argue in which contexts each model is more appropriate.

**Classification:**  
**A. Trigger words**

**Justification:**  
The prompt uses trigger words such as *compare*, *evaluate* and *argue*, which guide the model toward a structured and critical response.
